**3. 1918 Geographic Center of the Lower 48 States — Lebanon, Kansas** — *EarthStuff*
A historical-cartographic curiosity: in 1918 the U.S. Coast and Geodetic Survey, lacking the compute to integrate an irregular landmass, found the geographic center of the contiguous 48 by cutting the map out of cardboard and balancing it on a pin — accurate to within ±20 miles. A nice antidote to the day's algorithmic theme: the same problem, solved with a pin instead of a solver.
→ [Read on EarthStuff](https://earthstuff.substack.com/p/1918-geographic-center-of-the-contiguous)
