## Today's Themes

### 1. AI Transforms How We See (and Search) the Physical World

Two major product announcements landed today that together signal a pivotal shift in what geospatial AI can actually do. **Vexcel** launched [Vexcel Intelligence](http://eijournal.com/news/products-2/vexcel-intelligence-gives-ai-eyes-making-the-world-searchable-for-the-first-time), framing it as the first platform that lets AI systems truly search and reason about the physical world using continuous high-resolution aerial imagery — a move that positions Vexcel's image archive as foundational AI training infrastructure, not just a mapping product. Hours earlier, **Eagleview** released an [executive briefing](http://eijournal.com/news/products-2/eagleview-white-paper-warns-geospatial-intelligence-is-about-to-change-forever) warning that geospatial intelligence is entering its most consequential era, as conversational and agentic AI systems begin replacing traditional map-driven workflows with machine-speed decision-making. The two pieces read almost like a call and response — one company launching the thing the other is predicting.

---

### 2. Mapping What You Can't See: Underground Infrastructure Gets Serious Investment

**Groundhawk**, a Finnish startup, closed a [€2 million round](http://eijournal.com/news/business-2/groundhawk-raises-e2-million-to-scale-ai-powered-3d-underground-infrastructure-mapping-across-europe) to expand its AI-powered 3D underground infrastructure mapping platform across Europe. The pitch is compelling: centimetre-level accuracy captured in real time during active construction, turning every dig into a continuous survey and chipping away at the estimated €100 billion in annual global damage caused by inaccurate subsurface cable records. On the surface (literally), **Topcon** unveiled [new 3D machine control and geomatics innovations at CONEXPO 2026](https://www.geoconnexion.com/news/topcon-unveils-new-machine-control-safety-and-geomatics-innovations-at-conexpo-2026), reinforcing that the construction site as a continuously sensed, spatially accurate environment is becoming an industry expectation rather than a premium feature. These stories share an underlying logic: the last great frontier of geospatial accuracy isn't remote sensing from satellites — it's the infrastructure buried beneath our feet.

### 3. The Craft of Cartography, Alive and Well

Not everything today was about AI and venture capital. **John Nelson** published a [detailed tutorial](https://adventuresinmapping.com/2026/03/04/how-to-enthingify-maps-with-cartoon-imagery-and-chunky-overviews/) on what he calls "enthingifying" maps — layering cartoon-ized imagery, handsome terrain, touchable textures, and chunky overview globes to give map posters a tactile, physical presence. Elsewhere, **MapTiler** shipped a [major geocoding upgrade for North America](https://www.maptiler.com/news/2026/03/better-postal-code-coverage-and-accuracy-across-the-us-and-canada) — 10x more postal code coverage in Canada and meaningfully improved U.S. address accuracy — while the **ArcGIS Blog** walked through [building a cinematic scene with the ArcGIS Maps SDK for Unity](https://www.esri.com/arcgis-blog/products/unity/3d-gis/creating-a-cinematic-scene-with-arcgis-maps-sdk-for-unity). The throughline: geospatial visualization continues to mature far beyond the functional into the genuinely expressive.
